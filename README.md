Jamaluddin Jamali writes crossover book club fiction, suspense, and 
literary thrillers. His stories take you inside the darkest chambers

invisible corner, ready to fill your heart again.

### Paragraph 3:
He lives in Lahore with his wife and four children. He works as a 
reporter for City News Network, City42, and 24News. Previously, he 
wrote for three English newspapers: The Nation, The Post, and 
Pakistan Today. He holds a master's degree in English Literature 
and loves being close to nature — and animals, all of them, 
as manifestations of nature.

## 4. BOOK PAGE — The Sin of Killing

### Page Heading:
The Sin of Killing: The Dance of Changing Winds

### Tagline (bold):
"Our past is chasing us. We are on the run."

### Paragraph 1:
This novel takes you inside Pakistan — into its hidden backyard, 
into the soul of its society, its culture and family system. It 
shows how people fall in love and how sometimes that love becomes 
a death sentence, how divorced women and young widows are locked 
in the open-air prisons of society, with little chance of escape.

### Paragraph 2:
Young and beautiful Salma is celebrating her daughter's fifth 
birthday in Lahore when a single mad moment ends everything. 
Now in America, her nephew Ahsen carries a secret that could 
stop her heartbeat. Battling his family's hidden sins alone, 
his love gone, darkness closing in from all sides — he wonders 
what is right and what is wrong.

### Paragraph 3:
A tale of mismatched stars. Of love, loyalty, betrayal, 
redemption, and new beginnings. Of people finding new stars 
to live by, taking new starts again and again — for happiness, 
or for escape.

## 5. BOOK PAGE — The Baby Who Brought the Storm

### Page Heading:
The Baby Who Brought the Storm

### Tagline (bold):
"A Tear For Unmarried Mothers. A tribute."

### Paragraph 1:
A literary crossover of psychological realism set in Pakistan. 
Abandoned at birth near the gate of a public park, found and 
rescued by a Christian morning walker, Aadam is now the Managing 
Director of Hayat Group of Companies — raised by a wealthy Muslim 
family, grateful to his biological mother, carrying the weight of 
a society that raises other people's children but abandons its own.

### Paragraph 2:
When Aadam meets a sad and beautiful unmarried pregnant girl and 
decides to help her, it becomes his biggest mistake — one that 
ruins the family that raised him and threatens the love he has 
carried since school days.

### Paragraph 3:
A tale of bliss and sorrow, forbidden love, loyalty, betrayal, 
and social taboos. Several dream worlds, some broken, some intact. 
Aadam smiles when he should cry — and tries to save what matters most.
